# Research Audit: MiroFish & OpenClaw
**Status:** Massive Scale Simulation for Update 2.9 (The "Swarm" Pillar)  
**Reference:** [MiroFish Framework](https://github.com/marketingsales-debug/MiroFish) & [OpenClaw Always-On](https://github.com/OpenClaw/OpenClaw)

---

## 🧐 Conceptual Overview
Transition from "Multi-Agent Board" (3-5 agents) to **Massive Swarm Intelligence** (thousands to millions of agents). This layer provides the architecture for a "Digital Twin" of the internet, where agents with distinct personalities simulate global events.

## 🚀 Key Technical Insights

### 1. Swarm-Scale Forecasting (MiroFish)
*   **The Logic:** Spawns autonomous agents with unique memories and social connections to predict outcomes through emergent swarm behavior.
*   **Application:** Upgrade the **Simulation Engine**. Instead of 50 synthetic agents, MiroFish allows us to simulate a "Mini-Internet" of 10,000+ agents to test how a social narrative spreads across diverse demographics in 72 rounds.

### 2. Always-On Internet Mirroring (OpenClaw)
*   **The Logic:** An "Always-On" architecture that continuously ingests and responds to live data streams across multiple channels.
*   **Application:** The "Live Ingestion Heart." OpenClaw ensures our 10 platform scrapers (Reddit, Twitter, etc.) are unified into a single, persistent "Gateway" that the swarm can interact with 24/7.

### 3. Agent Personality & Social Graph
*   **The Logic:** Agents are not generic; they have "Personalities" and "Links" to other agents (based on MiroFish architecture).
*   **Application:** Enhances **Phase 7 (SocioVerse)**. We can now map our social fingerprints directly onto a massive MiroFish graph to see how specific influencers impact the swarm's collective "Tipping Point."

## 🤝 Strategic Integration for Update 2.9

1.  **Swarm Orchestrator:** Integration of MiroFish logic into `src/simulation/swarm.py`.
2.  **Persistent Gateway:** Deployment of OpenClaw as the middleware between scrapers and the agent board.
3.  **High-Capacity Simulation:** Migration of tournament logic to handle 1,000+ agents per prediction run.
